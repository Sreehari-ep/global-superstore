# global-superstore

## Overview
This dashboard provides a comprehensive view of global order shipments from the Global Superstore dataset. It enables users to analyze shipping patterns across countries, regions, and markets, with interactive filtering and visual summaries.
Dashboard Components
## Country Table 
- Purpose: Displays a list of countries with their associated region and market.
- Columns:
- Country: Name of the country (e.g., Afghanistan, Australia).
- Region: Geographical region (e.g., Central Asia, Oceania).
- Market: Business market classification (e.g., APAC, LATAM).
  ## Ship Mode Distribution 
- Title: Count of Order ID by Ship Mode
- Purpose: Visualizes the proportion of orders shipped via different modes.
- Categories:
- Standard Class: 60% of orders
- Second Class: 20.1%
- First Class: 14.63%
- Same Day: 5.27%
- Insight: Standard Class dominates shipping, indicating cost-effective logistics preference.
  ## Country Filter Panel 
- Purpose: Allows users to filter dashboard data by specific countries.
- Functionality: Selecting one or more countries updates the table and pie chart accordingly.
## Sum of Sales by City 
- Purpose: Highlights sales performance across major cities.
- Insight: New York leads in total sales, followed by Los Angeles, Manila, and Seattle.
- Use Case: Pinpoint urban centers driving revenue and assess city-level demand.
  ## Sum of Sales by Region 
- Purpose: Aggregates sales by broader geographic regions.
- Insight: Central region shows the highest sales, followed by South, North, and Oceania.
- Use Case: Evaluate regional performance for strategic planning and resource allocation.
  ## Sum of Sales by State 
- Purpose: Breaks down sales by state-level entities.
- Insight: England and California top the list, with strong contributions from Florida and New York.
- Use Case: Support localized marketing and inventory decisions.
  ## Sum of Sales by Market 
- Purpose: Compares sales across global market segments.
- Insight: Markets (APAC, EU, US, LATAM, EMEA, Africa, Canada) show relatively balanced performance.
- Use Case: Assess market saturation and identify opportunities for expansion.
